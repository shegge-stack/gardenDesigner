# Garden Layout & Design

## Raised Bed Dimensions

### Standard Sizes

| Size     | Area   | Best For                              |
|----------|--------|---------------------------------------|
| 4' x 4'  | 16 sq ft | Beginners, herbs, small gardens      |
| 4' x 8'  | 32 sq ft | Most popular; good variety of crops  |
| 4' x 12' | 48 sq ft | Serious gardener single-crop beds    |
| 3' x 6'  | 18 sq ft | Narrow spaces, children's gardens    |
| 2' x 8'  | 16 sq ft | Along fences, narrow side yards      |

### Critical Design Rules

- **Maximum width: 4 feet** — allows reaching center from either side without stepping in soil
- **If against wall/fence: maximum 2 feet wide** — access from one side only
- **Minimum depth: 6 inches** — functional but shallow
- **Recommended depth: 12-18 inches** — ideal for most vegetables
- **Deep root crops (carrots, potatoes): 18-24 inches**
- **ADA accessible: 24-36 inches high** with max 2' wide for wheelchair access

### Height Guidelines

| Height  | Use Case                          | Soil Volume (4x8)  |
|---------|-----------------------------------|---------------------|
| 6"      | On top of existing good soil      | 16 cubic ft         |
| 12"     | Standard; most crops              | 32 cubic ft         |
| 18"     | Deep root crops; bad native soil  | 48 cubic ft         |
| 24"     | Over concrete/poor drainage       | 64 cubic ft         |
| 30-36"  | ADA/standing height               | 80-96 cubic ft      |

### Materials

| Material        | Lifespan   | Cost    | Notes                              |
|-----------------|-----------|---------|-------------------------------------|
| Cedar           | 10-15 yrs | $$$     | Naturally rot-resistant             |
| Redwood         | 15-20 yrs | $$$$    | Premium, very durable               |
| Douglas Fir     | 5-7 yrs   | $$      | Budget option, shorter life         |
| Pine (untreated)| 2-4 yrs   | $       | Very short-lived                    |
| Galvanized steel| 20+ yrs   | $$$     | Modern look, heats soil             |
| Concrete block  | 30+ yrs   | $$      | Permanent, heavy                    |
| Stone/brick     | 30+ yrs   | $$$$    | Beautiful but labor-intensive       |

**WARNING:** Never use pressure-treated lumber manufactured before 2004 (contains CCA/arsenic). Modern ACQ-treated lumber is debated — most extension services say it's safe for edibles, but many gardeners prefer untreated cedar.

## Row Spacing (Traditional)

| Crop Category     | Between Plants | Between Rows | Notes              |
|-------------------|---------------|--------------|---------------------|
| Small (radish, carrot) | 2-4"     | 12-18"       | Can wide-row plant  |
| Medium (lettuce, beet) | 4-8"     | 18-24"       | Thin after emergence|
| Large (pepper, eggplant)| 18-24"  | 24-36"       | Stake if needed     |
| Very large (tomato)| 24-36"      | 36-48"       | Cage or stake       |

## Square Foot Gardening Method

### Setup
1. Build 4'x4' or 4'x8' raised bed, minimum 6" deep
2. Fill with Mel's Mix: 1/3 compost, 1/3 peat/coir, 1/3 vermiculite
3. Lay a grid (wood lath, string, or conduit) dividing bed into 1' squares
4. Plant each square according to spacing chart

### Plants Per Square Foot

| Category        | Plants/sq ft | Examples                           |
|-----------------|-------------|-------------------------------------|
| Extra-large     | 1/2 (2 sq ft) | Pumpkin, watermelon, winter squash|

| Medium-large    | 2           | Cucumber, head lettuce, cantaloupe|
| Medium          | 4           | Basil, Swiss chard, celery, parsley, corn, kohlrabi |
| Medium-small    | 6           | Leaf lettuce                       |
| Small           | 9           | Beets, spinach, turnips, garlic, leeks |
| Extra-small     | 16          | Radishes, carrots, green onions    |

## Sun Orientation

### Requirements
- **Full sun vegetables (6-8+ hours):** Tomatoes, peppers, corn, squash, beans, cucumbers, melons, eggplant
- **Partial sun OK (4-6 hours):** Lettuce, spinach, kale, chard, peas, beets, carrots, radishes, herbs
- **Shade tolerant (3-4 hours):** Lettuce, spinach, arugula, some herbs (mint, parsley, chives)

### Layout Rules (Northern Hemisphere)
1. **Orient beds north-south** for most even sun distribution
2. **Place tall crops on north/west side** of beds to prevent shading shorter plants
3. **Map sun patterns:** Track shadows from buildings, fences, trees throughout the day
4. **Minimum 6 hours direct sun** for a productive vegetable garden
5. **South-facing slopes** are warmest and earliest to warm in spring

### Sun Mapping
- Track sun at 9am, 12pm, 3pm in spring/summer
- Note shadow patterns from structures, trees, fences
- Account for deciduous vs. evergreen trees (leafout changes shadows)
- Garden placement should optimize June-August sun exposure

## Pathways & Access

| Path Type         | Width    | Purpose                           |
|-------------------|---------|-----------------------------------|
| Between beds      | 18-24"  | Walking access, kneeling          |
| Main garden path  | 36-48"  | Wheelbarrow, cart access          |
| ADA accessible    | 48" min | Wheelchair passage                |

### Path Materials
- Wood chips/bark mulch (cheapest, needs annual replenishment)
- Gravel (durable, good drainage)
- Pavers/flagstone (permanent, expensive)
- Straw (temporary, cheap, decomposes)
- Cardboard + mulch (weed suppression)

## Key Calculations for Design Tool

```
# Raised bed soil volume
soil_cu_ft = length_ft * width_ft * (height_in / 12)
soil_cu_yd = soil_cu_ft / 27
# Approximate cost: $30-60 per cubic yard bulk soil/compost

# Mel's Mix quantities for a 4x8x12" bed
total_cu_ft = 4 * 8 * 1 = 32
peat_cu_ft = 32 / 3 = 10.7
vermiculite_cu_ft = 32 / 3 = 10.7
compost_cu_ft = 32 / 3 = 10.7

# Lumber for raised bed (4x8x12" with 2x12 boards)
# 2 boards @ 8' (sides), 2 boards @ 4' (ends) = 24 linear feet of 2x12
# Plus corner posts: 4 @ 12" of 4x4

# Number of plants in SFG bed
total_squares = length_ft * width_ft
# Each square plants based on spacing chart
```
