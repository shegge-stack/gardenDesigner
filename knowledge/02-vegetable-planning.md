# Vegetable Garden Planning

## Companion Planting

### Research-Backed Beneficial Pairings

| Combination                          | Benefit                                            | Evidence Level |
|--------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|----------------|
| Basil + Tomatoes                     | Reduces thrips; may promote tomato growth          | Research-backed |
| Marigolds + Tomatoes                 | Reduces thrips populations                         | Research-backed |
| Nasturtium + Squash/Pumpkins         | Reduces squash bugs                                | Research-backed |
| Sage + Thyme + Brussels Sprouts      | Reduces diamondback moths                          | Research-backed |
| Thyme + Onion + Nasturtium + Broccoli| Reduces cabbage looper/worm                        | Research-backed |
| Marigold + Onion + Nasturtium + Cabbage | Reduces cabbage worms                           | Research-backed |
| Arugula/Mustard as trap crop + Brassicas | Flea beetles prefer trap crop (3+ species best)| Research-backed |
| Blue Hubbard squash trap crop        | Attracts cucumber beetles away from other cucurbits| Research-backed |
| Buckwheat/Cowpea/Sunn hemp + Corn    | Increases corn earworm predators/parasitoids       | Research-backed |
| African marigolds + Sweet alyssum    | Increases natural aphid enemies                    | Research-backed |
| Corn + Beans + Squash (Three Sisters)| Structural support, N-fixation, weed suppression   | Traditional/proven |
| Legumes (any) + Heavy feeders        | Legumes fix atmospheric nitrogen in soil           | Well-established |

### Disproven/Unsupported Pairings

| Claimed Combination             | Claimed Benefit              | Reality                           |
|---------------------------------|------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Marigolds + Potatoes            | Repel Colorado potato beetle | Multiple studies show no effect   |
| Marigolds + Brassicas           | Repel flea beetles           | Little research to support        |
| Green onions + Brassicas        | Repel flea beetles           | Little research to support        |

### Space-Efficiency Pairings
- **Succession in same space:** Lettuce/spinach/basil (spring) → Peppers/tomatoes (summer)
- **Interplanting by root depth:** Radishes (shallow) + Carrots (deep) in same row
- **Vertical + ground:** Corn (tall) + squash (ground cover) + beans (climbing)

**Source:** [UMN Extension - Companion Planting](https://extension.umn.edu/planting-and-growing-guides/companion-planting-home-gardens)

## Crop Rotation

### Plant Families for Rotation

| Family          | Latin Name      | Members                                                    | Feeding Level |
|-----------------|----------------|------------------------------------------------------------|---------------|
| Nightshades     | Solanaceae      | Tomatoes, peppers, eggplant, potatoes, tomatillos          | Heavy feeder  |
| Brassicas       | Brassicaceae    | Broccoli, cabbage, cauliflower, kale, Brussels sprouts, radishes, turnips, kohlrabi, collards, bok choy, arugula, rutabaga | Heavy feeder |
| Cucurbits       | Cucurbitaceae   | Cucumbers, squash, pumpkins, melons, watermelon, zucchini, gourds | Heavy feeder |
| Legumes         | Fabaceae        | Beans, peas, lentils, peanuts, soybeans, fava beans        | Light (N-fixer) |
| Alliums         | Liliaceae       | Onions, garlic, leeks, shallots, chives, scallions         | Light feeder  |
| Umbellifers     | Apiaceae        | Carrots, parsnips, celery, dill, cilantro, parsley, fennel | Light feeder  |
| Goosefoots      | Chenopodiaceae  | Spinach, beets, Swiss chard                                | Moderate      |
| Composites      | Asteraceae      | Lettuce, endive, radicchio, artichoke, sunflower           | Light feeder  |
| Grasses         | Poaceae         | Sweet corn, popcorn                                        | Heavy feeder  |

### 4-Year Rotation Plan

| Year | Bed A          | Bed B          | Bed C          | Bed D          |
|------|----------------|----------------|----------------|----------------|
| 1    | Legumes        | Brassicas      | Nightshades    | Cucurbits      |
| 2    | Brassicas      | Nightshades    | Cucurbits      | Legumes        |
| 3    | Nightshades    | Cucurbits      | Legumes        | Brassicas      |
| 4    | Cucurbits      | Legumes        | Brassicas      | Nightshades    |

**Key rules:**
- Never follow nightshades with nightshades (shared diseases: blight, verticillium wilt)
- Never follow nightshades with strawberries (shared verticillium wilt)
- Plant brassicas AFTER legumes (benefit from nitrogen fixation)
- Wait minimum 3 years before same family returns to same bed
- Alliums, umbellifers, goosefoots, and composites fit into any rotation year as secondary crops

**Source:** [Penn State Extension - Plant Rotation by Families](https://extension.psu.edu/plant-rotation-in-the-garden-based-on-plant-families)

## Succession Planting

### Strategy
Plant the same crop every 2-3 weeks to extend harvest window instead of getting one large harvest.

### Best Crops for Succession Planting in Zone 4

| Crop         | Sow Every | Season Window           | Notes                        |
|--------------|-----------|-------------------------|------------------------------|
| Lettuce      | 2 weeks   | April-August             | Bolts in heat; shade in summer |
| Radishes     | 2 weeks   | April-May, Aug-Sept      | Skip midsummer (too hot)     |
| Spinach      | 2 weeks   | April-May, Aug-Sept      | Bolts in heat               |
| Beans (bush) | 3 weeks   | May 15-July 15           | Need warm soil               |
| Peas         | 2 weeks   | April-May only           | Heat intolerant              |
| Carrots      | 3 weeks   | April-June               | 65-80 days to harvest        |
| Beets        | 3 weeks   | April-July               | Tolerate light frost         |
| Cilantro     | 2 weeks   | April-September          | Bolts quickly in heat        |

### Fall Harvest Planting
After harvesting early crops (lettuce, radishes, peas, spinach), replant beds with:
- **Late June-July:** Broccoli, Brussels sprouts, cabbage, cauliflower (transplants)
- **July-August:** Beets, carrots, kale, lettuce, radishes, turnips, spinach

**Source:** [UMN Extension - Midsummer Planting for Fall Harvest](https://extension.umn.edu/planting-and-growing-guides/planting-vegetables-midsummer-fall-harvest)
