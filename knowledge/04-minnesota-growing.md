# Minnesota-Specific Growing Guide

## Growing Season Overview

| Region              | Zone  | Season Length | Last Frost    | First Frost   |
|--------------------|-------|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| Northern MN         | 3b    | 110-130 days  | May 15-25     | Sept 10-20    |
| Central MN          | 4a    | 130-150 days  | May 5-15      | Sept 20-30    |
| Twin Cities Metro   | 4b    | 140-165 days  | Apr 28-May 10 | Sept 25-Oct 8 |
| Southern MN         | 5a    | 150-175 days  | Apr 20-May 5  | Oct 1-15      |

**Typical gardening season: May through September**

## Minnesota Planting Calendar (Zone 4b — Twin Cities)

### Indoor Seed Starting Schedule

| Weeks Before Transplant | When (Zone 4b)     | Start These Seeds                              |
|------------------------|--------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| 14-16 weeks            | January-Early Feb  | Onions, leeks, celery                          |
| 10-12 weeks            | Late Feb-Early Mar | Broccoli, cabbage, cauliflower, head lettuce, peppers |
| 8-10 weeks             | Mid-March          | Eggplant, Brussels sprouts                     |
| 5-6 weeks              | Mid-April          | Tomatoes, basil, zinnias, cosmos               |
| 3-4 weeks              | Late April         | Cucumbers, melons, squash (optional)           |

**Source:** [UMN Extension - Starting Seeds Indoors](https://extension.umn.edu/planting-and-growing-guides/starting-seeds-indoors)

### Seed Starting Conditions
- Soil temperature for germination: keep above 60°F (most seeds)
- Bottom heat mats raise soil temp ~5°F above ambient
- Light requirement: 12-16 hours daily from LEDs/fluorescents
- Light distance: no more than 4 inches above seedling tops
- Harden off 1-2 weeks before transplanting

### Outdoor Planting Timeline (Zone 4b)

**Mid-April (cool-season transplants & direct sow):**
- Transplant: Broccoli, Brussels sprouts, cabbage (early), cauliflower, collards, head lettuce, onions
- Direct sow: Peas, spinach, radishes, lettuce, potatoes
- Plant: Asparagus crowns, rhubarb, horseradish

**Mid-April to Mid-May:**
- Direct sow: Carrots, beets, kohlrabi, leaf lettuce (continue through June)
- Potatoes, radishes

**Mid-May (after last frost — tender crops):**
- Transplant: Tomatoes, peppers, eggplant, basil
- Direct sow: Beans, corn, cucumbers, squash, pumpkins, melons, watermelon
- Plant: Sweet potatoes (slips)

**Early June (late warm-season):**
- Transplant: Late broccoli, late cabbage, late cauliflower, peppers, eggplant

**Late June to August (fall harvest planting):**
- Direct sow: Beets, carrots, kale, lettuce, radishes, spinach, turnips
- Transplant: Broccoli, Brussels sprouts, cabbage for fall

**Source:** [UMN Extension - Planting the Vegetable Garden](https://extension.umn.edu/planting-and-growing-guides/planting-vegetable-garden)

## What Grows Well in Zone 4

### Best Performers (reliable, productive)
- **Tomatoes** — short-season varieties (Early Girl, Glacier, Sub Arctic Plenty, Juliet)
- **Peppers** — sweet varieties do well; hot peppers need warm microclimate
- **Beans** (bush and pole) — very reliable
- **Peas** — excellent spring crop, plant early
- **Cucumbers** — productive with adequate warmth
- **Zucchini / summer squash** — extremely productive
- **Kale** — thrives in cool weather, frost-tolerant
- **Lettuce / greens** — spring and fall staple
- **Root crops** (carrots, beets, radishes, turnips) — very reliable
- **Potatoes** — excellent Minnesota crop
- **Onions / garlic** — garlic planted in fall, onions from transplants
- **Herbs** — basil, dill, cilantro, parsley, chives (perennial)

### Challenging but Possible
- **Melons** — need warmest microclimate, black plastic mulch, short-season varieties
- **Watermelon** — same as melons; small varieties (Sugar Baby, Blacktail Mountain)
- **Sweet potatoes** — need 90+ days warm soil, black plastic helps
- **Eggplant** — needs heat; wall-o-water or similar protection helps
- **Winter squash / pumpkins** — long season; start indoors or choose short-season varieties
- **Corn** — possible but needs large block planting for pollination

### Not Recommended for Zone 4
- Artichokes (perennial type — Zone 7+)
- Okra (needs sustained heat, marginal)
- Long-season sweet potatoes (choose short-season)
- Figs, citrus (not hardy)

## Season Extension Techniques

### Soil-Warming Mulches

| Mulch Type                    | Soil Temp Increase | Notes                          |
|------------------------------|--------------------|---------------------------------|
| Black plastic                | +5°F               | Suppresses weeds too           |
| Clear plastic                | +14°F              | Best warming but weeds grow under |
| IRT (infrared-transmitting)  | +10-12°F           | Weed suppression + good warming |
| Red plastic                  | Variable           | May improve tomato/pepper yields|

### Protective Structures

**Row Covers (Floating):**

| Weight   | Frost Protection | Light Transmission | Primary Use                |
|----------|------------------|--------------------|----------------------------|
| Heavy    | Down to ~20°F    | 50-60%             | Hard frost protection      |
| Medium   | Down to ~28°F    | 75-85%             | Early season crop protection|
| Light    | Minimal          | 90%+               | Insect exclusion           |

**Cold Frames:**
- Standard size: 4' wide x 4' deep x 2' high
- Construction: plywood sides + old window sash or polycarbonate top
- Extends season 4-6 weeks on each end
- Vent when interior exceeds 70°F to prevent overheating
- Use for hardening off seedlings, growing cool-season crops earlier/later

**Low Tunnels:**
- Wire hoops (9-10 gauge) spaced every 3 feet
- Insert hoops 6 inches into soil
- Cover with row cover fabric or plastic
- Good for extending harvest of cold-hardy crops into November

**High Tunnels (Hoop Houses):**
- Semi-permanent structures with metal tubing frame
- Covered in greenhouse plastic (6 mil, 4-year UV-rated)
- Extends season by 6-8 weeks each end
- Can enable year-round growing of cold-hardy greens in Zone 4
- Requires venting for temperature management

**Water-Filled Protectors (Wall-O-Water):**
- Allow transplanting warm-season crops 3 weeks earlier
- Water cylinders absorb heat during day, release at night
- Protect individual plants to ~20°F
- Best for tomatoes and peppers

**Source:** [UMN Extension - Extending the Growing Season](https://extension.umn.edu/planting-and-growing-guides/extending-growing-season)

## Minnesota Growing Calendar Summary (Zone 4b)

| Month     | Key Activities                                                    |
|-----------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| January   | Plan garden, order seeds, start onion/leek seeds late month     |
| February  | Start onions, leeks, celery indoors; plan layout                |
| March     | Start brassicas, peppers indoors; soil test                     |
| April     | Start tomatoes mid-month; direct sow peas/spinach/radish outdoors; prepare beds |
| May       | Transplant warm crops after May 10-15; direct sow beans, corn, squash; succession plant lettuce |
| June      | Plant fall brassica transplants late month; weed, water, mulch  |
| July      | Direct sow fall crops (beets, carrots, kale); succession plant; harvest begins |
| August    | Peak harvest; last succession sowings; direct sow late radish/lettuce/spinach |
| September | Harvest continues; protect tender crops from early frost; plant garlic late month |
| October   | Final harvest; clean up beds; mulch garlic; plant cover crops   |
| November  | Put garden to bed; cold frame greens may still produce          |

**Source:** [UMN Extension - Garden Care Calendar](https://extension.umn.edu/planting-and-growing-guides/upper-midwest-home-garden-care-calendar)
