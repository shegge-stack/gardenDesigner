# Pest & Disease Management

## Integrated Pest Management (IPM) Principles

IPM uses a hierarchy of strategies to manage pests with minimal environmental impact:

1. **Prevention** — crop rotation, resistant varieties, clean garden practices
2. **Cultural controls** — proper spacing, watering, mulching, timing
3. **Mechanical/physical** — hand-picking, row covers, traps, barriers
4. **Biological** — beneficial insects, Bt, nematodes
5. **Chemical (organic)** — neem, spinosad, pyrethrin, kaolin clay (last resort)

**Source:** [UMN Extension - Preventing Pests](https://extension.umn.edu/how/preventing-pests-your-yard-and-garden)

## Common Minnesota Vegetable Garden Pests

### Brassica Pests (Broccoli, Cabbage, Kale, Cauliflower)

| Pest                  | Generations/yr | Peak Season       | Damage                         | Organic Controls                                    | Threshold          |
|-----------------------|---------------|-------------------|---------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|--------------------|
| Cabbage Maggot        | 1-3           | May-early June    | Larvae feed on roots           | Row covers at planting; large transplants; delay planting to late May | Preventive only    |
| Flea Beetle           | 1-3           | May-early June    | Adults chew small holes in leaves | Row covers; trap crops (arugula, mustard — use 3+ species); transplants | 10-20% leaf damage |
| Imported Cabbage Worm | 1-3           | Midsummer         | Larvae eat holes in leaves     | Row covers; hand-pick; Bt; Spinosad                  | 20-30% plants infested |
| Diamondback Moth      | 4-6           | Early summer      | Larvae eat holes in leaves     | Row covers; scout/destroy eggs; Bt; Spinosad; beneficial insects | Scout and spray    |
| Cabbage Looper        | 3-5           | Midsummer (migrates)| Larvae eat holes in leaves   | Row covers; Bt; Spinosad                             | With cabbage worm  |

### Cucurbit Pests (Cucumber, Squash, Melon, Pumpkin)

| Pest                  | Generations/yr | Peak Season       | Damage                         | Organic Controls                                    | Threshold          |
|-----------------------|---------------|-------------------|---------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|--------------------|
| Striped Cucumber Beetle| 1            | At planting       | Chew leaves; transmit bacterial wilt | Row covers until flowering; kaolin clay; blue hubbard trap crop; silver mulch; transplants not direct seed | 1 beetle/plant (cucumber, melon) |
| Squash Bug             | 1            | Early-mid summer  | Suck sap causing wilting       | Destroy egg clusters; knock nymphs into soapy water; clean fields; tolerant varieties (sweet cheese pumpkin, royal acorn) | Scout weekly       |
| Squash Vine Borer      | 1            | Early-mid summer  | Larvae tunnel through stems    | Row covers until flowering; crop rotation; yellow bucket traps; Bt injection into stem; tolerant varieties (C. moschata, cucumbers, melons) | Preventive         |

| Pest              | Season        | Damage                    | Organic Controls                              |
|-------------------|---------------|---------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|

## Organic Pest Control Products

| Product           | Target Pests                     | Mode of Action              | Notes                          |
|-------------------|----------------------------------|-----------------------------|--------------------------------|
| Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis) | Caterpillars (worms, loopers) | Bacterial stomach poison | Safe for beneficials; must be ingested |
| Spinosad          | Caterpillars, thrips, beetles    | Nervous system toxin        | Derived from soil bacteria; toxic to bees when wet |
| Neem oil          | Aphids, whiteflies, mites        | Growth disruptor, repellent | Apply evening to avoid bee contact |
| Pyrethrin         | Broad spectrum knockdown         | Nervous system toxin        | Breaks down quickly in sunlight; toxic to bees and fish |
| Kaolin clay       | Cucumber beetles, flea beetles   | Physical barrier on leaves  | Must reapply after rain        |
| Insecticidal soap | Soft-bodied insects (aphids, mites)| Dissolves insect cuticle  | Direct contact only; safe for beneficials |
| Diatomaceous earth| Slugs, crawling insects          | Abrasive to exoskeleton     | Ineffective when wet           |
| Iron phosphate    | Slugs, snails                    | Stomach poison              | Safe around pets and wildlife  |

## Common Garden Diseases (Minnesota)

### Fungal Diseases

| Disease           | Crops Affected            | Symptoms                          | Management                                   |
|-------------------|--------------------------|-----------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Early Blight      | Tomatoes, potatoes       | Brown spots with concentric rings | Crop rotation; mulch; remove lower leaves; resistant varieties |
| Late Blight       | Tomatoes, potatoes       | Water-soaked lesions, white mold  | Destroy infected plants immediately; copper fungicide preventive |
| Powdery Mildew    | Squash, cucumbers, peas  | White powdery coating on leaves   | Resistant varieties; spacing for airflow; neem oil; baking soda spray |
| Downy Mildew      | Cucumbers, lettuce       | Yellow spots, gray mold underneath | Resistant varieties; morning watering; remove infected leaves |
| Fusarium Wilt     | Tomatoes, peppers        | Yellowing, wilting from base up   | Resistant varieties (F); crop rotation; solarize soil |
| Verticillium Wilt | Tomatoes, eggplant, strawberries | Yellowing, wilting one side | Resistant varieties (V); rotation; don't follow nightshades with strawberries |
| Septoria Leaf Spot| Tomatoes                 | Small dark spots with gray centers | Mulch; remove lower leaves; copper fungicide   |
| Clubroot          | Brassicas                | Swollen, distorted roots         | Raise pH to 7.2+; long rotation (7 years); resistant varieties |

### Bacterial Diseases

| Disease           | Crops Affected      | Symptoms                    | Management                              |
|-------------------|--------------------|-----------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Bacterial Wilt    | Cucumbers, melons  | Sudden wilting, sticky sap  | Control cucumber beetles (vectors); resistant varieties; remove infected plants |
| Fire Blight       | Apples, pears      | Blackened, curled shoot tips | Prune 12" below infection; sterilize tools |
| Bacterial Spot    | Tomatoes, peppers  | Water-soaked spots on leaves/fruit | Copper spray preventive; resistant varieties; avoid overhead water |

### Physiological Problems (Not Disease)

| Problem               | Crops           | Cause                              | Prevention                      |
|-----------------------|-----------------|------------------------------------|---------------------------------|
| Blossom End Rot       | Tomatoes, peppers| Calcium deficiency from irregular watering | Consistent watering; mulch; calcium amendment if soil deficient |
| Sunscald              | Tomatoes, peppers| Direct sun on fruit                | Don't over-prune; shade cloth   |
| Blossom Drop          | Tomatoes, peppers| Temperature stress (too hot/cold)  | Wait for temperatures to moderate; shade cloth in heat |
| Bolting               | Lettuce, spinach, cilantro | Heat/long days trigger flowering | Plant cool-season crops early; succession plant; shade cloth |
| Cracking (fruit/roots)| Tomatoes, carrots | Irregular watering              | Consistent watering; mulch      |

## Disease Prevention Best Practices

1. **Crop rotation** — 3-4 year minimum for same plant family
2. **Resistant varieties** — look for V, F, N, T, A codes on tomato labels
3. **Air circulation** — proper spacing, pruning, trellising
4. **Water management** — drip irrigation (keeps foliage dry); water morning
5. **Mulch** — prevents soil splash onto lower leaves
6. **Sanitation** — remove diseased plant material; don't compost diseased plants
7. **Tool hygiene** — clean tools with 10% bleach between diseased plants
8. **Don't work wet plants** — fungi and bacteria spread easily on wet foliage
