### Bloom Sequence Design
For continuous pollinator support, plant species that bloom in succession:
- **Spring (May-June):** Wild columbine, prairie smoke, blue flag iris
- **Early Summer (June-July):** Milkweeds, wild bergamot, coneflower
- **Mid Summer (July-Aug):** Black-eyed Susan, Joe Pye weed, blazing star
- **Late Summer/Fall (Aug-Oct):** Goldenrod, New England aster

**Source:** [UMN Extension - Plants for Pollinators](https://extension.umn.edu/lawns-and-landscapes/flowers-pollinators), [MN DNR Pollinator Resources](https://www.dnr.state.mn.us/pollinator_resources_tables/index.html)

## Rain Gardens

### Sizing Formula
- Rain garden area = **1/3 the impervious surface area** draining into it
- Typical size: 100-300 sq ft
- Depth: 4-10 inches (bottom must be level)
- Soil must absorb water within 48 hours

### Infiltration Test
1. Dig a 10-inch hole in proposed location
2. Fill with water
3. Must drain completely within 2 days
4. If not, amend soil or choose different location

### Rain Garden Plant Zones

**Zone 1 — Bottom (tolerates standing water):**
- Blue flag iris, sedges, bulrush, cardinal flower

**Zone 2 — Middle slope (moist to occasionally wet):**
- Swamp milkweed, Joe Pye weed, blue lobelia, boneset

**Zone 3 — Upper edge (drier, well-drained):**
- Black-eyed Susan, coneflower, blazing star, little bluestem

### Native Grasses for Rain Gardens
- Switchgrass (36-72", moist)
- Little bluestem (24-36", dry-medium)
- Indian grass (36-72", medium-moist)
- Prairie dropseed (24-36", dry-medium)

**Source:** [UMN Extension - Rain Gardens](https://extension.umn.edu/landscape-design/rain-gardens)

## Shade Gardens (Zone 4)

### Shade Types
- **Full shade:** <3 hours direct sun (north side of buildings, dense trees)
- **Part shade:** 3-6 hours direct sun (filtered light through deciduous canopy)
- **Dappled shade:** Shifting sunlight through leaf canopy all day

## Foundation Plantings (Zone 4)

### Evergreen Shrubs
- **Boxwood** (some cultivars to Zone 4) — 2-4', formal
- **Arborvitae** — 3-15', columnar or globe forms
- **Mugo Pine** — 3-5', mounded
- **Juniper** (various) — groundcover to upright forms
- **Yew (Taxus)** — 3-6', shade tolerant (poisonous berries)

### Deciduous Shrubs
- **Hydrangea (paniculata types)** — Zone 3-4, 4-8'
- **Ninebark** — 4-8', native, peeling bark interest
- **Dogwood (red/yellow twig)** — 6-8', winter stem color
- **Viburnum** — 6-10', berries for birds
- **Lilac** — 6-12', fragrant spring bloom
- **Spirea** — 2-5', easy care, various bloom colors

### Foundation Design Rules
1. Mature plant width should be 1/2 the distance from house to walkway edge
2. Space plants at 1/2 to 2/3 of their mature width apart
3. Plant at least 2-3 feet from foundation walls (roots, moisture, access)
4. Mix evergreen + deciduous for year-round interest
5. Shorter plants in front, taller in back
6. Consider mature height vs. window placement

**Source:** [UMN Extension - Trees and Shrubs](https://extension.umn.edu/trees-and-shrubs/trees-and-shrubs-minnesota-landscapes)
